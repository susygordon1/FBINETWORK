The Three Pillars
What We Bring to the Table
F
Faith

Scripture. Prayer requests. Answered prayers. Encouragement. Share whatever you're carrying or whatever you've seen God do. This is knowledge sharing — bring anything faith has taught you.

B
Business

Tools, wins, referrals, hard lessons. What are you using? What's working? What do you need? The table is open. We grow each other's businesses by sharing what we know.

I
Impact

One goal. One act. This week, you commit to making an impact in your community — and next Monday you report back. Buy someone's lunch. Give someone a ride. Show up for someone who needs it.

What Makes FBI Different
"FBI is not a networking event. It's not a pitch room. There are no door prizes, no card shuffles, no pressure to sell."

The format serves one thing: that every person in the room feels seen, heard, and supported — in their faith, in their business, and in their impact on the world around them.

If you're looking for a room full of people who will show up for you the same way you show up for them — this is it.

The Meeting
One Hour. Two Blocks. Every Monday.
Minutes 0–30
The Briefing
  • 30-Second Commercial Who are you and what do you do? Own it.
  • Faith What are you bringing? Prayer need, scripture, something God showed you. Open table.
  • Business Tools, wins, knowledge, referrals. What do you know that this room needs to hear?
  • Impact Your one community goal for the week. Specific. Actionable. Report back next Monday.
Minutes 30–60
FBI's Most Wanted
  • The Speaker One subject matter expert. Thirty minutes. Lived experience only — no theory.
  • The Topic Could be a business skill, a legal lesson learned, a tool that changed everything. Real and practical.
  • The Close Every speaker closes with a scripture. Then passes the baton to next week's Most Wanted.
  • The Contribution We pass the hat for the FBI cause. Kingdom-minded giving, no pressure.
